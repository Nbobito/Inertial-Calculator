To use this calculator, simply draw your cross-section in the grid above. Each pixel represents one balsa wood strip, measuring 3/32 of an inch.

This calculator assumes that the bending axis occurs in the center of the cross-section, meaning that you'll need to adjust your rows and columns in order to make your shape fill to the edges of the grid. The results have been scaled by 1000 to help with readability, so be sure to divide by 1000 before using these in equations. Results are also colored by column, so just because one cell is greener doesn't mean it has the highest value; it just means it has the highest value for that column.

Left-click on a calculated table entry to put it back on the grid, and right-click to remove it.

That's just about it. Try drawing some shapes like rectangles or I-beams and see how their different inertias and ratios compare.
